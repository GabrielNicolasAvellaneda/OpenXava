The basic unit over OpenXava build applications are called Business Components.

A Business component, or OpenXava component, stores all related information about a business concept.
Suppose the business concept Invoice, all related information about one Invoice it's defined inside the Invoice.xml file. You define all validations, stored data, business logic, graphical interface, tabular data, mapping to databases, etc.

Usually to develop J2EE applications the developers teams make a split between different technical roles: graphical designer, business logic programmers, deployers (to configure the application), etc. Opposite to this point of view OpenXava propose a split based on the functionality and OpenXava has to solve technical aspects (graphical interface, data access , etc. ) We have verified that this way to manage our teams are more efficient that the older ones.

The Business Component approach is an alternative to the traditional MVC frameworks. In MVC the code is classified in the Model (the business logic), the View (the User Interface) and the Controller (the behaviour). In OpenXava the code is classified in Invoice, Delivery, Customer, etc. That is, it's classified using business concepts as base.
